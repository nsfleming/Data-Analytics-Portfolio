2023 Higher Education Analytics Data Competition

The project assessed the lack of flexibility in public transit services provided to persons with disabilities in the City of Hamilton. At the time, the door-to-door service provided by a contractor was limited to subscribed trips for recurring medical appointments or other activities, and casual trips that were reserved seven days in advance. The city was interested in exploring opportunities to improve the overall customer experience for clients using the accessible transportation services (ATS).

The project aimed to convert trip data from ATS into useful information that could provide insights into the service. The project explored the potential to offer more flexibility to persons with disabilities by identifying areas for improvement. The insights generated from the data helped to address key needs such as understanding the patterns of trips made by clients, the major trip destinations, trips by ward, inter-regional trips, volume by time and day, and the impact of the future Light Rail Transit Corridor.

Additionally, the project assessed the opportunities to bring rural clients to the urban transit boundary and onto conventional HSR transportation, and the possibility of creating community bussing in some areas.

The reasons why the project needed to be carried out were due to the city's commitment to improving the overall customer experience for clients using public transit. The city recognized the importance of providing accessible transportation services to persons with disabilities and the need to ensure that the service was flexible, efficient, and met the needs of its clients. The city also recognized the potential benefits that the conversion of trip data into useful information could have on improving the service.

Pokemon Data Analysis

This is an EDA of Pokemon statistics, which involves the analysis of a dataset containing information on 721 unique Pokemon from the first generation Kanto Region to the 6th generation Kalos Region Pokemon X and Y. The dataset includes various fields such as ID, Name, Type 1, Type 2, Total, HP, Attack, Defense, SP Atk, SP Def, Speed, Generation, and Legendary. The objective of the analysis is to identify which features have an impact on a Pokemon's Total stats. The first step involves importing the required libraries and reading the data into R. The custom color palette is defined for the Pokemon types to facilitate data visualization. The dataset is then explored, and the variables and data types are identified using various R functions. Summary statistics of the dataset are also checked.
